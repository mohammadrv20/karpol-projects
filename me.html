Early Life (c. 570 CE - 610 CE)

  • Born into a merchant family in Mecca (now in Saudi Arabia) around 570 CE.
  • Orphaned at a young age, raised by his grandfather and uncle.
  • Known for his honesty and trustworthiness, earning the nickname "al-Sadiq" (the truthful).
  • Worked as a merchant, traveling throughout the Arabian Peninsula.
  • Married Khadijah, a wealthy widow, and became a respected member of Meccan society.

    • The Revelation (610 CE)

  • At the age of 40, Muhammad experienced a pivotal moment in a cave near Mecca.
    According to Islamic tradition, he received the first revelation from God (Allah) through the angel Gabriel.
  • These revelations would continue for the next 23 years, forming the basis of the Quran, the holy book of Islam.

    • Preaching Islam and Facing Opposition (610 CE - 622 CE)


  • Muhammad began preaching Islam, a monotheistic religion that emphasized social justice, compassion, and submission to God's will.
  • His teachings challenged the polytheistic practices of the Meccan elite, who faced a potential loss of power and prestige.
  • Muhammad and his followers faced persecution and ridicule from the Quraysh tribe, the ruling class of Mecca.

    The Hijra (Migration) and Building a Community (622 CE - 630 CE)

  • In 622 CE, fearing for the safety of his followers, Muhammad led a migration (Hijra) from Mecca to Medina, a city about 270 km north.
  • In Medina, Muhammad established the first Muslim community, laying the foundation for Islamic social and political structures.
  • Muslims and Meccans clashed in several battles, with the Muslims eventually emerging victorious.

    • Return to Mecca and Final Years (630 CE - 632 CE)

  • In 630 CE, Muslims marched on Mecca, and the city surrendered peacefully.
    Muhammad destroyed the idols in the Kaaba, a cubical structure revered by Arabs, and rededicated it to the worship of Allah.
  • Islam gained momentum throughout Arabia. Muhammad performed the Farewell Pilgrimage, establishing the rituals of Hajj for future generations.
  • He died in Medina in 632 CE, leaving behind a legacy that continues to shape the world today.
